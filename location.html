Right above us?

This is where the ISS is right now!

The giant floating laboratory has been travelling at five miles per second since it was launched.

The International Space Station orbits the earth 15.54 times a day.

The ISS serves as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory in which crew members conduct experiments in biology, human biology, physics, astronomy, meteorology, and other fields.

The ISS is the largest human-made body in low Earth orbit and can often be seen with the naked eye from Earth.
